About

I’m a PhD student at the Department of Chemical Engineering and Biotechnology, University of Cambridge supervised by Sebastian Ahnert.

My research is applying network science methods for biological networks, probing the interface between structure and function. I’m working on connectomics, gene regulatory networks and protein secondary structure contact maps.

In the future, I'm interested in investigating dynamics on networks and dynamical models of networks. For neuroscience, this would mean modelling brain networks across behavioural, developmental and evolutionary time scales.
